In 1575, the seigneury of Versailles was bought by Albert de Gondi, who invited Louis XIII on several hunting trips in the forests surrounding Versailles.

In 1687 Jules Hardouin Mansart built the Grand Trianon, probably the most refined group of buildings anywhere in the domain of Versailles

Italian architecture heavily influenced the architecture of the building, which stands between a courtyard and garden.

Mansart was inspired by Italians.

Day 1

Transfer from Florence airport to the Villa Cora. Relax and explore Florence’s city center. Welcome dinner.

Day 2

Meet the chef for first hands-on cooking class. Lunch to follow based on our efforts. A three-hour walking tour in the Oltrarno, an ancient neighborhood of Florence, which includes a visit to the gardens of Boboli and Bardini. At the end of the tour, see a sunset at Ponte Vecchio and then head to dinner at Trattoria il Parione.

Day 3

Depart with our guide for Cortona to explore the old Etruscan village. Visit the winery Avignonesi, home of some of the most prestigious Montepulciano red wines. Dinner at “le Logge del Vignola” in Montepulciano, a medieval village.

Day 4

Piazza Santa Maria Novella for a food/art tour. Watch Dominican friars make soap and perfumes. Stop at a local “forno” bakery to taste focaccia with olive oil and a pizza slice straight from the oven. The tour ends in the Sant’Ambrogio district and the fruit and vegetable market in a 19th-century building. Second cooking class. Make dinner.

Day 5

Third cooking class. Afternoon is free to explore Florence. Choice in dining options: enjoy a seafood dinner at Chef Daniele Pescatore’s restaurant “Il Cenacolo” or an authentic Florentine experience by sharing a dinner with a family in a private home.
